Data Privacy & Regulatory Compliance

Summary:
Data privacy compliances including GDPR become a priority with growing global enforcement and regulations projected to impact data flows and international business operations.

Full Brief:
Companies are increasingly embracing the Data Privacy Framework (DPF), with numbers growing to 2800 organizations, driven by the enforcement of the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and a rise in privacy laws globally ("Trump Administration Playing Truth or Dare", [**Infosecurity Magazine**](https://www.infosecurity-magazine.com/opinions/trump-eu-us-data-privacy-framework/)). Other regions such as China have unique regulatory expectations such as the Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL) that has its own rigorous data transfer compliance procedures. Meanwhile, Ireland's powerful privacy regulator delivered a significant blow to TikTok with a ‚Ç¨530 million fine, emphasizing the necessity to adhere to GDPR ("Why TikTok ruling sparks trouble", [**politico.eu**](https://www.politico.eu/article/tiktok-fine-eu-china-relations-ireland-data-privacy-regulation-surveillance/)). The research director at the International Association of Privacy Professionals emphasized the strategic implications of these developments, stating, "Regulatory, geopolitical, and industry developments are carving the world up into greenlisted, redlisted, and firewalled blocs for data sharing" ("Why TikTok ruling sparks trouble", [**politico.eu**](https://www.politico.eu/article/tiktok-fine-eu-china-relations-ireland-data-privacy-regulation-surveillance/)). Microsoft also recently appointed a European Deputy Chief Information Security Officer, indicating a serious effort to catch up with compliance regulations, including the Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA), the NIS 2 Directive, and the Cyber Resilience Act (CRA).

Key Themes:
- More organizations are joining the Data Privacy Framework, possibly due to increased enforcement of GDPR and other privacy laws ("Trump Administration Playing Truth or Dare", [**Infosecurity Magazine**](https://www.infosecurity-magazine.com/opinions/trump-eu-us-data-privacy-framework/)).
- China's data privacy regulations, particularly the PIPL, set unique and stringent requirements for global business operations.
- Heavy fines, like Ireland's ‚Ç¨530 million penalty against TikTok, are reshaping the way companies approach data transfer, making it a front and center issue ("Why TikTok ruling sparks trouble", [**politico.eu**](https://www.politico.eu/article/tiktok-fine-eu-china-relations-ireland-data-privacy-regulation-surveillance/)).
- Compliance with new regional regulations like the DORA, NIS 2 Directive, and CRA is proving to be crucial in a global business context. Microsoft's appointment of a dedicated role for European compliance highlights this ([**SecurityWeek**](https://www.securityweek.com/rsa-conference-2025-announcements-summary-part-3)).

Consulting & Advisory Trends

Summary:
Industry professionals unite under Up& Consulting, while client acquisition becomes a team effort in firms with a global digital strategy driving transformation.

Full Brief:
This week has seen major strides towards a more collaborative business approach in the consulting and advisory industry. IBE Consultancy, KSA Strategy, and GECS have joined forces to create Up& Consulting. This new, multidisciplinary consultancy brings together industry professionals to offer 360-degree strategic solutions, from digital transformation to organizational design (**openPR**, **https://www.openpr.com/news/3995331/ibe-consultancy-ksa-strategy-gecs-unite-as-up-consulting**). These developments come part and parcel with an industry-wide shift towards digital transformation, with a unified strategy being essential (**Accounting Today**). This is illustrated by ACT Education breaking their seven-year Lenovo spree in a device refresh (**iTnews**, **https://www.itnews.com.au/news/act-education-breaks-seven-year-lenovo-spree-in-device-refresh-616856**).

Key Themes:
- Industry professionals are collaborating to offer well-rounded strategic solutions in consulting and advisory sectors (**openPR**).
- Client acquisition is being approached as a team effort, reflecting a modernized approach to business development (**Accounting Today**).
- There's a definitive shift towards digital transformation across different sectors, with unified strategies being crucial (**Accounting Today**).
- ACT Education's recent update in technology represents a trend in organizations adapting to keep up with the digital shift (**iTnews**).

Financial Services Transformation

Summary:
WealthTech is playing catch-up in the digital transformation of the financial services sector, with collaboration and blockchain technology seen as crucial elements for progress in this area.

Full Brief:
While banking, payments, and Regtech have made significant strides in digital transformation, Wealthtech has been described as the "last part of financial services to really go through digital transformation" (**Finextra Research**, **https://www.finextra.com/newsarticle/45925/ifgs-2025-is-it-time-for-wealthtech-to-take-the-stage**). Industry leaders believe that wealth management services need to incorporate new technologies across their entire value chains, and collaboration with leading technology is suggested as a winning strategy for those in Wealthtech. The deployment of blockchain technology is also widely viewed as critical to the digital transformation in this sector, acting as the framework for an open and transparent system that more closely aligns with next-generation values. Additionally, AI continues to offer opportunities for enhanced fraud prevention, authorization rates, and personalized customer engagement. These transformative processes are integral to achieving operational efficiency and meeting compliance requirements, particularly with sophisticated AI systems that adapt and update to variations and changes without requiring reprogramming.

Key Themes:
- Wealthtech is behind other financial sectors in digital transformation, but new technologies across the value chain and partnerships with leading tech can propel progress in this area (**Finextra Research**, **https://www.finextra.com/newsarticle/45925/ifgs-2025-is-it-time-for-wealthtech-to-take-the-stage**).
- Blockchain technology is deemed critical for the digital transformation in Wealthtech, turning a traditionally opaque system into an open, transparent, and inclusive one (**Finextra Research**, **https://www.finextra.com/newsarticle/45925/ifgs-2025-is-it-time-for-wealthtech-to-take-the-stage**).
- AI-driven financial solutions offer significant opportunities for improved fraud prevention, higher authorization rates, risk management, and personalized customer service. AI's capability to adapt to changes allows quick response to new compliance requirements while maintaining operational efficiency.
